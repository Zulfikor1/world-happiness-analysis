# World Happiness Report 2024 - Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

This project performs an exploratory data analysis (EDA) on the World Happiness Report 2024 dataset, focusing on understanding global happiness levels and their relationships with economic and social factors.

## Dataset
- **Source**: [World Happiness Report 2024 on Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ajaypalsinghlo/world-happiness-report-2024)
- **Format**: CSV
- **Columns**:
  - Country name
  - Ladder score (happiness score)
  - Explained by: Log GDP per capita
  - Explained by: Social support
  - Explained by: Healthy life expectancy
  - Explained by: Freedom to make life choices
  - Explained by: Generosity
  - Explained by: Perceptions of corruption
  - Dystopia + residual

## Steps
- Loading and inspecting the dataset
- Handling missing values
- Finding the top 10 happiest countries
- Finding the bottom 10 least happy countries
- Analyzing the relationship between GDP per capita and happiness

## Visualizations
- **Top 10 Happiest Countries** (bar chart)
- **Bottom 10 Least Happy Countries** (bar chart)
- **GDP per Capita vs Happiness Score** (scatter plot)

All graphs are saved in the `/images/` folder.

## Results
- Finland, Denmark, and Iceland are among the happiest countries.
- Afghanistan and Lebanon rank among the least happy countries.
- Higher GDP per capita generally correlates with higher happiness scores.
